When choosing between building something from scratch and composing on top of an existing native/proven capability, the user's tiebreaker is **"what provides higher quality"** — and they lean toward composition unless reconstruction demonstrably wins.

**Why:** In the `nxtlvl` session they asked *"what provides higher quality"* twice as the literal decision criterion, accepted "compose on native — and it isn't close" for orchestration, and rescoped to *"use agent-skills as our foundation but refine/enhance"* rather than rebuild.

**How to apply:** Frame build-vs-reuse decisions by leading with the quality comparison, and recommend composing on the platform/native layer or a strong reference unless you can show reconstruction yields higher quality. Note the nuance: they still want to *own and refine* what they compose on (vendor-and-refine), not call it untouched. Relates to [[nxtlvl-harness]].
